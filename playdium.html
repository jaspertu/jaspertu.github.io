    Playdium

    Connecting kids while physically distanced

    Jasper Tu • 2 min read

    Background

    A project completed for the Nickelodeon + Adobe XD Creative Jam, winning 3rd place. The design prompt was to design a kids' game to connect them amidst the pandemic and educate them on COVID safety.

    My Contribution

    I took part in research, ideation, wireframing, and transitioning our screens into hi-fi. I also contributed to mapping out the user journey, design system and helped with prototyping some animations. Some of my focus points were the Avatar customization flow, in-game flow, and figuring out how the controls would be laid out in-game (+ what controls to include in the first place).

    What is Playdium?

    Grade 6 to 8 kids enjoy being physically connected through fun, outdoor games. However, this positive experience has now been taken away due to COVID-19. From being locked up at home, user research revealed 11–13 year olds often suffer from increased anxiety, nightmares, and meltdowns. Hence, we digitized manhunt—one of their favourite games—to let 11–13 year olds spark joy, relieve stress, and enlighten them on best health practices.

    Playdium cheers up lonely children stuck at home by letting them personalize their own character, theme their game worlds with Nickelodeon shows, and most importantly, connect with friends and family through manhunt within a rich, 3D world. Simultaneously, it facilitates learning of important health and safety knowledge pertaining to COVID-19; if one gets “tagged”, they are challenged to answer questions correctly in a time limit. Enriching the game experience are items which give temporary power-ups.
