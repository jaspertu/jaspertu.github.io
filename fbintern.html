    What Did I Work On?

    The Brand Collabs team is part of the Facebook App Monetization (FAM) pillar. They focus on facilitating the creation of quality branded content, working at the intersection of brand-creator relationships. Branded content is defined as posts made by a creator for payment by a business partner (brand), who influences the content or is featured in it. A key problem creators face is experiencing a mismatch in pricing expectations when negotiating a potential deal with brands. Therefore, I designed a Pricing Preferences feature to improve creator transparency about pricing needs to brands, so they can be matched with the right projects.

    The Concept + Rationale

    ü§ë Why Pricing Preferences? üí∏

    Pricing Preferences enables creators to input their preferred minimum compensation into Creator Studio; this is then shown on Creator Portfolios in BCM, where advertisers search for suitable candidates who can star in upcoming branded content projects. 2 key value propositions are offered by this approach:

    For creators, facilitate accurate matching. Align creators with the right projects at price points they desire, increasing monetization opportunities.

    For Facebook, develop a baseline. Enable Facebook to gather information to form a POV on how branded content value is determined.

    With a concept, I designed an MVP prototype. I explored different interaction design patterns for creator price selection like cards, sliders, and dropdowns.

    An issue with cards was the idea of adding and removing Content Types. Did removing a content type mean the creator no longer created that medium or didn‚Äôt want to specify a price? Due to ambiguity in connotation between the different states, the cards concept was scrapped.

    An issue with sliders was lack of granularity when the user chooses a desired price‚Äîif the range is too wide, accuracy in selection is lost. It is also difficult to pinpoint what an ideal end price would be, since both small and large creators need to be considered.

    Intuitive + Simple

    Dropdowns

    I ultimately decided on dropdowns because it had the most opportunity to create an intuitive experience. Dropdowns let users easily select various states for each content type (Photo, Video, Stories). Being able to set different preferences is key because research revealed prices tend to vary by content type and not every creator makes branded content in every medium. Here are the different states:

    üîë Pricing available by request [Default State]

    Brand needs to personally contact creator for pricing info; creator doesn‚Äôt wish to share pre-contact.

    üîë Set starting price

    Lets creators set a per post pricing floor by content type.

    üîë Content type not offered

    Creators specify to brands they don‚Äôt create branded content for a certain medium.
